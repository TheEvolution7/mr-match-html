 Introduction to the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

At Mr.Match, we are committed to protecting and respecting your privacy. The GDPR, effective as of May 25, 2018, is a pivotal law under EU legislation that aims to strengthen and unify data protection for all individuals within the European Union (EU). It sets forth rigorous new rules on data privacy, which we are fully committed to complying with, in order to ensure that your personal information is safe, secure, and handled with the utmost care.

17. Why GDPR Matters

17.1 The GDPR is designed to: Enhance Privacy Rights: The regulation provides EU residents with greater control over their personal data and assurances that their information is being securely protected across Europe.

17.2 Simplify the Regulatory Environment: By unifying data protection regulations within the European Union, GDPR simplifies the regulatory environment for both businesses and individuals.

17.3 Increase Credibility and Trust: Compliance helps ensure that organizations are operating transparently and in the best interests of the data they manage.

18. Key GDPR Principles We Follow

18.1 Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency: Processing of personal data must be lawful, fair, and transparent to the data subject.

18.2 Purpose Limitation: Data is collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes and not further processed in a manner that is incompatible with those purposes.

18.3 Data Minimization: The collection of data is adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary in relation to the purposes for which they are processed.

18.4 Accuracy: Every reasonable step must be taken to ensure that personal data that are inaccurate are erased or rectified without delay.

18.5 Storage Limitation: Personal data are kept in a form which permits identification of data subjects for no longer than is necessary for the purposes for which the personal data are processed.

18.6 Integrity and Confidentiality: Personal data are processed in a manner that ensures appropriate security of the personal data, including protection against unauthorized or unlawful processing and against accidental loss, destruction, or damage.

19. Your Rights Under the GDPR

As a data subject under the GDPR, you possess several significant rights:

19.1 Right to Access: You have the right to access your personal data and to receive information about its use.

19.2 Right to Rectification: You can have your information corrected if it is inaccurate or incomplete.

19.3 Right to Erasure (‘Right to be Forgotten’): You can request the deletion or removal of your personal data when there is no compelling reason for its continued processing.

19.4 Right to Restrict Processing: You have rights to 'block' or suppress further use of your data.

19.5 Right to Data Portability: You have the rights to obtain and reuse your personal data for your own purposes across different services.

19.6 Right to Object: You have the right to object to the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances, including direct marketing.
